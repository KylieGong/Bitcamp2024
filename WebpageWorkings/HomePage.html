Maryland Waste Disposal

Importance of Why We Separate Waste

Food waste is a sustainability issue that the state of Maryland must address. This website allows users to interact with state-wide and local policies to inform YOU on how to be a more sustainably engaged citizen. Furthermore, reducing waste also decreases the amount of waste sent to landfills and incinerators and reduces the environmental impact of land used for landfills, the contamination caused by landfills, and pollution caused by waste incineration. Waste can be separated into three general categories: landfill/incineration, recycling, and compost. Miscellaneous items that have specialized disposal methods include batteries, electronic waste, and large items such as furniture. Waste disposal is a complex system that involves state and local governments. Incorrectly sorted items can cause economic impacts. It is important to empower local communities to act pro-environmentally. Our mission is to help educate and empower Marylanders to increase recycling and composting through self tracking systems and easy-to-access information.
